## 3. The OOMs Framework — Complete Treatment

The OOMs (Orders of Magnitude) framework is Aschenbrenner's core analytical tool. It makes AI progress — and therefore the required capex — **predictable**.

### 3a. Compute Scaling (~0.5 OOMs/year)

Training compute for frontier AI models has been growing at approximately 0.5 orders of magnitude per year — meaning each year, the largest training runs use roughly 3x more compute than the year before.

- **The trendline**: From GPT-2 (2019) to GPT-4 (2023), training compute grew by roughly 4 OOMs (10,000x)
- **What this means physically**: Clusters are scaling from ~100,000 GPU equivalents (2024) to potentially 100 million GPU equivalents by 2030
- **The cost curve**: $10 billion clusters (2024) -> $100 billion clusters (2026-2027) -> $1 trillion clusters (2028-2030)
- **"Over the past year, the talk of the town has shifted from $10 billion compute clusters to $100 billion clusters to trillion-dollar clusters. Every six months another zero is added to the boardroom plans."**
- **Investment implication**: Every doubling of cluster size means more GPUs, more power, more cooling, more networking, more data center space. The capex trendline is not slowing — it is accelerating.

### 3b. Algorithmic Efficiency (~0.5 OOMs/year)

Independent of compute scaling, researchers are finding ways to do more with less compute:

- Algorithmic improvements have historically delivered ~0.5 OOMs/year of equivalent compute gains
- This means a model trained today with the same compute as GPT-4 would be significantly more capable due to better training methods
- By 2027, models may benefit from 2-3 additional OOMs of algorithmic efficiency — equivalent to 100-1,000x more effective compute at the same hardware cost
- Key sources of algorithmic gains: better architectures, improved training objectives, data curation, mixture-of-experts, distillation techniques
- **"Trust the trendlines... The magic of deep learning is that it just works — and the trendlines have been astonishingly consistent, despite naysayers at every turn."**

### 3c. Unhobbling (From Chatbots to Agents)

Aschenbrenner borrowed the term from horse training — hobbling means tying a horse's legs to keep it docile. Current AI models are **massively hobbled**:

- **They don't think before they speak**: "When you ask ChatGPT to write an essay, that's like expecting a human to write an essay via their initial stream-of-consciousness."
- **They can only do short dialogues**: Rather than going away for a day or a week, thinking about a problem, researching different approaches, and then writing a longer report.
- **They lack tools and agency**: Limited ability to browse the web, run code, query databases, or interact with the physical world.

Key unhobbling breakthroughs:
- **RLHF** (Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback): Made models instruction-following and commercially useful
- **Chain-of-Thought**: Unlocked step-by-step reasoning, dramatically improving math and logic performance
- **Tool use**: Gave models access to calculators, code interpreters, web browsers
- **Scaffolding and agentic architectures**: Multi-step planning, self-critique, iterative refinement
- **Long context windows**: From 4K tokens to 100K+ tokens, enabling complex document analysis
- **System 2 reasoning**: Extended thinking, where models generate millions of tokens internally before responding

**"Unhobbling is a huge part of what actually enabled these models to become useful — and I'd argue that much of what is holding back many commercial applications today is the need for further unhobbling of this sort. Indeed, models today are still incredibly hobbled!"**

The transition from chatbot to agent is the single biggest unlock: **"By 2027, rather than a chatbot, you're going to have something that looks more like an agent, like a coworker."**

### 3d. The "Intelligence Explosion" Endgame

This is Aschenbrenner's most profound and controversial claim:

- **"We don't need to automate everything — just AI research."**
- Hundreds of millions of AGIs could be deployed simultaneously to work on AI improvement

**Timeline**: By 2027-2028, AGI arrives. By 2028-2029, the intelligence explosion is underway. By 2030, superintelligence.

### 3e. Why OOMs Make AGI Timeline Predictable

The power of the OOMs framework is that it turns a seemingly unpredictable question ("When will we get AGI?") into a quantitative one:

- GPT-2 to GPT-4: ~preschooler to ~smart high-schooler in 4 years (roughly 4 OOMs of effective compute)
- At the current rate (~1 OOM/year combining compute + algorithmic efficiency + unhobbling), another 3-4 OOMs by 2027 is achievable
- 3-4 more OOMs beyond "smart high-schooler" plausibly reaches expert-level or beyond
- **This is not a prophecy — it is a trendline extrapolation backed by a decade of consistent data**

## 4. The Bottleneck Hierarchy — Expanded

### Layer 1: Power (The Hardest Constraint)

This is Aschenbrenner's most differentiated and investment-relevant insight. **Power is the ultimate bottleneck because it is the hardest to scale quickly.**

**The scale of the problem:**
- A 2024 frontier training cluster: ~100 MW (equivalent to a small city)
- A 2026 cluster: ~1 GW (equivalent to the Hoover Dam or a large nuclear reactor)
- A 2030 trillion-dollar cluster: ~100 GW (over 20% of total US electricity production)
- **Total US electricity generation has barely grown 5% in the last decade.** AI alone demands tens of percent growth this decade.

**Why power is harder than compute:**
- You can manufacture GPUs in 6-12 months; building a power plant takes 3-7 years
- Transmission infrastructure (lines, substations, transformers) takes even longer and faces permitting nightmares
- Power purchase agreements (PPAs) are being signed years in advance; the competition is fierce
- **"Behind the scenes, there's a fierce scramble to secure every power contract still available for the rest of the decade, every voltage transformer that can possibly be procured."**

**Energy source analysis:**
- **Natural gas**: The fastest path to scale. A 10 GW cluster would take only a few percent of US natural gas production. A 100 GW cluster would need ~1,200 new wells. But tech companies have climate commitments that constrain this.
- **Nuclear**: The cleanest long-term solution. SMRs (Small Modular Reactors) are being explored, but timelines are long. Companies like Constellation Energy (existing nuclear fleet) benefit from the demand for clean baseload power.
- **Solar + batteries**: Scalable but intermittent. Requires massive battery storage to provide reliable baseload for 24/7 data center operations.
- **Fuel cells**: Bloom Energy's fuel cell technology can be deployed faster than traditional power plants, making it a bridge solution. This explains its position as Aschenbrenner's largest holding by Q4 2025.
- **Geothermal**: Promising but unproven at scale.

**Investment implications:**
- Independent power producers (IPPs) with existing capacity have immense pricing power
- Nuclear operators with existing fleets are irreplaceable
- Companies that can deploy power quickly (fuel cells, portable generation) command premiums
- Grid infrastructure (transformers, switchgear, cables) faces multi-year order backlogs
- **The power bottleneck is self-reinforcing**: the longer it takes to solve, the higher the value of existing capacity

### Layer 2: Compute Infrastructure (GPUs, Data Centers, Networking, Cooling)

- GPUs/ASICs remain the computational engine, but supply is increasing faster than power
- Data center operators who already have **power interconnection agreements** and **permits** are extremely valuable
- **Bitcoin miners pivoting to AI**: This is Aschenbrenner's key alpha insight. Companies like Core Scientific, IREN, Cipher Mining, Riot Platforms, and Bitdeer already hold electrical permits from their mining days. They have power capacity that would take years for new entrants to obtain.
- Optical networking (Lumentum) for high-bandwidth interconnects between GPU clusters
- Liquid cooling becomes essential as power density per rack increases
- Cloud GPU providers (CoreWeave) who are building dedicated AI infrastructure

### Layer 3: The Model Layer (Commoditizing)

- Aschenbrenner explicitly de-emphasizes picking the winning AI lab
- The model layer is commoditizing: open-source models (DeepSeek R1, Llama) prove that algorithmic techniques diffuse quickly
- What matters is not who has the best model today, but who has access to the physical infrastructure to train the next generation
- Some private exposure (e.g., Anthropic connections) but not the core thesis
- **"The question isn't which model wins — it's whether the infrastructure gets built."**

### Layer 4: The Application Layer (Too Early)

- AI revenue must eventually justify the capex, but the capex happens FIRST
- Application-layer winners are unpredictable and subject to model commoditization risk
- The industrial mobilization happens regardless of which application wins
- Investing in applications before the infrastructure is built is premature

### How Bottlenecks Shift Over Time

- **2024-2025**: GPU supply is the binding constraint (Nvidia dominance)
- **2025-2027**: Power and data center capacity become binding as GPU supply increases
- **2027-2030**: The trillion-dollar cluster era — power becomes the dominant bottleneck
- **Post-AGI**: If the intelligence explosion occurs, the bottleneck may shift to robotics, energy production, and physical-world manufacturing
- **Key principle**: Invest in the NEXT bottleneck before the market reprices. By the time the market consensus identifies a bottleneck, it's already priced in.

## 5. The Geopolitical Framework

### 5a. US-China AI Race Dynamics

Aschenbrenner frames the AI race as the defining geopolitical contest of the 21st century:

- **"If we're lucky, we'll be in an all-out race with the CCP; if we're unlucky, an all-out war."**
- AGI represents military and economic dominance comparable to — or exceeding — nuclear weapons
- China is not out of the game despite export controls: **"Once the CCP wakes up to AGI it has a clear path to being competitive."**
- The race creates an imperative for speed on the US side, which accelerates capex and benefits infrastructure plays
- China's AI progress (demonstrated by DeepSeek) actually increases US spending urgency

### 5b. Export Controls — Effectiveness and Leakiness

- US export controls on advanced chips aim to slow China's AI progress
- Aschenbrenner acknowledges they are **leaky** — chips are diverted through third countries, and algorithmic efficiency partially compensates
- But controls still impose a meaningful compute tax on Chinese labs
- The key question is whether controls can maintain a sufficient lead during the critical 2025-2028 window
- **Investment implication**: Companies on the right side of export controls benefit; those with heavy China exposure face regulatory risk

### 5c. "The Project" — Government AGI Mobilization

This is one of Aschenbrenner's most distinctive predictions:

- By 2027-2028, as AGI capabilities become undeniable, the US national security state will fully awaken
- **"It's an insane proposition that the US government will let a random SF startup develop superintelligence."**
- Predicted sequence: Leading labs will be forced to merge or coordinate under government oversight; Congress will appropriate trillions for compute clusters; core AGI research teams will move to secure facilities
- **"By late 2026/27/28, the core AGI research team (a few hundred researchers) will move to a secure location; the trillion-dollar cluster will be built in record-speed; The Project will be on."**
- This represents the largest government procurement program since the Manhattan Project
- **Investment implication**: Massive government AI spending benefits infrastructure providers, defense contractors, and energy companies positioned for government contracts

### 5d. Nationalization and Security Risks

- **"Authoritarians could use superintelligence for world conquest, and to enforce total control internally. Rogue states could use it to threaten annihilation."**
- AI labs currently treat security as an "afterthought" — making them vulnerable to state-level espionage
- The CCP will attempt to infiltrate American AI labs "with billions of dollars and thousands of people"
- Model weights and algorithmic secrets are the crown jewels; physical security of data centers becomes a national security priority
- Potential risk: outright nationalization of AI labs or forced partnerships with government entities

### 5e. Friend-Shoring and Allied Supply Chains

- Aschenbrenner explicitly warns against building data centers in semi-friendly countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia): **physical access enables theft**
- "Once you put them in authoritarian dictatorships, you create this irreversible security risk"
- Clusters must be located in the US and allied democracies
- This benefits semiconductor supply chains in allied nations: Japan, South Korea, Taiwan, Netherlands, Israel
- Ivanka Trump described Aschenbrenner's memo as "an excellent and important read" — indicating bipartisan policy influence
- His thinking has influenced congressional bills aimed at "keeping American AI out of China's hands"

## 7. The DeepSeek Response (January 2025) — A Masterclass in Thesis Resilience

- Their reasoning: Even if China makes algorithmic breakthroughs, this INCREASES demand for US compute and power — US labs need to stay ahead, which means MORE capex, not less
- Cheaper models mean more usage, which means more inference compute demand
- DeepSeek validates the algorithmic efficiency OOM — it doesn't invalidate the compute scaling OOM

**The ripple effect:**
- One major tech fund reportedly held back from dumping shares after an analyst on their team said simply: **"Leopold says it's fine."**
- He was right — the market recovered, and his positions in power and infrastructure continued to appreciate

**Investment lesson**: When the market panics on AI narratives, check whether your bottleneck thesis is intact. If the physical constraints haven't changed, the selloff is an opportunity. DeepSeek didn't reduce the need for electricity, data centers, or cooling — if anything, it increased it by demonstrating that AI progress is faster than expected, which accelerates the timeline for massive infrastructure buildout.

## Quick-Reference: The Situational Awareness Investment Checklist

When evaluating any stock through the Aschenbrenner framework:

1. **Is this company positioned at a bottleneck in the AI buildout?** Power > Compute > Models > Applications. The scarcer the resource, the better the pricing power.

2. **Does this company benefit regardless of which AI lab wins?** "Picks and shovels" beats "gold miners." Avoid dependency on a single model or platform.

3. **What's the capex trajectory?** Are hyperscalers increasing or sustaining AI capex? Every dollar flows to someone in the supply chain.

4. **Is the market pricing in the full magnitude of the AI buildout?** Most analysts still assume capex peaks soon. Aschenbrenner believes it accelerates through the decade.

5. **What's the geopolitical exposure?** Export controls, friend-shoring, supply chain security. Does this company benefit from or get hurt by US-China decoupling?

6. **Is there optionality if AGI timelines accelerate?** Some investments pay off in the base case but have enormous convexity if AGI arrives sooner. Options structures capture this asymmetry.

7. **Can this company deploy capacity FAST?** In the bottleneck era, speed of deployment is a competitive advantage. Companies that can deliver power, compute, or infrastructure faster than competitors command premium valuations.

8. **Is management aligned with the thesis?** Look for companies actively pivoting toward AI infrastructure (e.g., Bitcoin miners converting to AI hosting) rather than passively hoping to benefit.
